## I believe my rights were violated by law enforcement

- Write down everything they remember, including officers' badges and patrol car numbers, which agency the officers were from, and any other details. Get contact information for witnesses.
- If injured, seek medical attention immediately and take photographs of injuries.
- File a written complaint with the agency's internal affairs division or civilian complaint board. In most cases, complaints can be filed anonymously.
- Information on filing a complaint against ICE agents can be found here .

The ACLU of North Carolina might be able to help if an individuals' constitutional rights were violated. Fill out our legal intake form to notify us of your case.

## Schools

- Guidance for Schools Explains protections for immigrant students under FERPA and constitutional law. Advises on handling ICE presence, safeguarding student information, and implementing policies to protect students and families.
- Checklist for ICE Arrival Serves as a guide on how to handle ICE interactions before they arrive, during, and after an encounter.
- University Guidance Advice for students, faculty, or staff who may be impacted by immigration enforcement on campus.
- Open Letter to College &amp; University on Harboring Liability + ICE Administrative Subpoenas Addressing significant questions that may arise regarding colleges' and universities' rights and obligations in the face of certain Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) investigations and enforcement actions. Immigration Administrative Subpoenas This document lays out several steps that recipients can take in response to an ICE subpoena.

## Places of Worship

- Guidance for Places of Worship Outlines legal rights and limitations regarding immigration enforcement, including warrant requirements and sanctuary declarations. Offers recommendations on protecting congregants, like defining private areas and training staff.
- Checklist for ICE Arrival Serves as a guide on how to handle ICE interactions before they arrive, during, and after an encounter.
